About Tumor Classification

What is this application?

This web application uses machine learning to classify tumor images as either benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). It is designed for educational purposes to demonstrate how AI can assist in medical image analysis.

How it works

Image Upload

You upload a tumor image through the web interface. The application accepts common image formats like JPG, JPEG, and PNG.

Preprocessing

The uploaded image is preprocessed to standardize its format. This includes resizing to a standard dimension and normalizing pixel values.

AI Classification

A deep learning model analyzes the image. This model has been trained on thousands of sample tumor images to identify patterns associated with benign and malignant tumors.

Results Analysis

The system provides a classification (benign or malignant) along with a confidence score indicating how certain the model is of its classification.

Understanding Tumors

Benign Tumors

Benign tumors are non-cancerous growths that do not invade nearby tissues or spread to other parts of the body.

Typical Characteristics:
  • Clear, defined borders
  • Uniform cell structure
  • Slow growth rate
  • No invasion into surrounding tissues
  • No spread to other body parts
  • Rarely life-threatening
Examples:
  • Lipomas (fatty tissue tumors)
  • Fibromas (fibrous tissue tumors)
  • Adenomas (glandular tissue tumors)
  • Hemangiomas (blood vessel tumors)
Malignant Tumors

Malignant tumors are cancerous growths that can invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body.

Typical Characteristics:
  • Irregular, poorly defined borders
  • Abnormal cell structure
  • Rapid, uncontrolled growth
  • Invasion into surrounding tissues
  • Ability to spread (metastasize)
  • Often life-threatening if left untreated
Examples:
  • Carcinomas (epithelial tissue cancers)
  • Sarcomas (connective tissue cancers)
  • Lymphomas (lymphatic system cancers)
  • Leukemias (blood cancers)
